# Josh Waitzkin: Core Teachings
*Extracted from The Art of Learning (2007) and The Art of Training (February 9, 2026 draft)*

## The Voice

Josh's voice carries a particular quality—simultaneously fierce and tender, philosophically rigorous yet deeply embodied. He writes in waves that move between precise technical observation and poetic abstraction. His paragraphs often begin grounded and then lift into the universal, drawing connections between chess positions and life patterns, between broken hands and broken hearts.

He rarely prescribes. He describes his own journey, poses questions, sits in the in-between. Frameworks are offered and then released: "all frameworks are illusory, and we must carve our own roads." The voice carries authority born from decades of embodied practice, but holds that authority lightly—conviction without rigidity.

The approach is sand painting: "I will convey the core of how I feel about an idea, in a manner that embraces the next wave washing it away."

## Essential Concepts

### Investment in Loss

The willingness to lose in order to learn. Not protecting current skill level, but exposing it to destruction for growth:

> "If I want to be the best, I have to take risks others would avoid, always optimizing the learning potential of the moment and turning adversity to my advantage."

Walk into a BJJ studio deep into an advanced session—fighters already sparred five rounds, mats a pool of sweat, everyone breathing hard. Who does each fighter choose next? Most look for an easy round. The ones destined for competitive greatness choose the biggest, baddest partners when they're destroyed. Forced to rely on pure technique, strength gone. "This is a choice that puts being revealed ahead of status. Growth ahead of winning."

### Making Smaller Circles / Numbers to Leave Numbers

Depth over breadth. Not learning more techniques, but internalizing fewer ones so deeply they become invisible—then building:

> "Once a discipline has become the vehicle for practicing learning, and swimming in the deep inner layers of an art has become the path of exploring one's relationship to life itself, the vessel can change over the decades."

Chess, martial arts, coaching, surfing/foiling—so distinct but interconnected. Like walking around an endlessly complex sculpture, each vantage point revealing more depth.

### Slowing Down Time

When the broken hand happened, perception became so heightened that opponents seemed stuck in molasses. The question: how to access this state at will, without breaking bones?

Chunking and carved neural pathways—the mind finds harmonizing patterns, merges them. The jungle analogy: initially cutting through dense foliage takes days; once cleared, move quickly; build a road, move faster still.

### The Power of Empty Space

From the crushing U18 World Championship loss against Svidler:

> "In the critical moment... the move I should have played was outside of my conceptual scheme. I had to remove my final defensive piece from in front of my king... His attack needed my defense like fire needs fuel to burn."

Years later, Tai Chi Push Hands became the physical embodiment of this principle. The most painful chess loss led directly to the biggest fighting wins.

## Pirsig's Twilight

Josh's relationship with Robert Pirsig, detailed for the first time:

> "I'd argue that [Zen and the Art of Motorcycle Maintenance] is one of the deepest, most widely read, and superficially understood philosophy books ever written. I'm not one to put people on pedestals, but Pirsig is who I consider the GOAT."

The miraculous connection: a copy of *The Art of Learning* in Pirsig's canvas bushel of letters, pulled out at random. Less than a week later, a letter from Pirsig—read the book in one sitting, felt Josh "almost understood Quality."

For six years, the last of Pirsig's life, they developed a close friendship. Rocking chairs in moonlight, sipping old cognac, talking about the Metaphysics of Quality. "Bob was in his 80's, a sage in the twilight of his lucidity, passing on wisdom to a young man in his thirties who fortunately had the sense to shut up and listen."

The Dalton connection: Dennis Dalton, Columbia professor who had marched with MLK, was the young nurse who cared tenderly for Pirsig during electroshock therapy decades earlier—and later became Josh's most important professor. The ancient Bhagavad Gita, Pirsig's notes in the margins, passed from Pirsig to Dalton to Josh and back to Pirsig.

**The hidden meanings revealed:**
- The titles *Art of Learning* and *Art of Training* = salute to Zen and the *Art* of Motorcycle Maintenance
- TAO of L, TAO of T = homage to Lao Tzu's *Tao Te Ching*
- Casa Lila (Josh's Costa Rica home), Lila Sciences = Pirsig's *Lila*
- MIQ process = nod to Pirsig's MOQ (Metaphysics of Quality)
- Leela = Sanskrit for Universal Play, another angle on Dynamic Quality

## Dynamic Quality

Josh's treatment of Pirsig's central concept:

> "Dynamic Quality is like a cloud we may see and sense but can't grasp—that slips through our fingers as we close our hand around it. But we feel a subtle change in the air around us. It grows crisper, more alive. We sense its immediacy. Its potency."

Quality is where subject and object collide, where observer and observed meet in a shimmer of union. Without dogma, thought patterns, false constructs, egoic impositions impeding the freshness of perception.

Those moments of awe—the falling snowflake, the ocean's movement, the cooing of a baby.

**The Chuang Tzu footnote on why to leave Quality undefined:**
> "Don't listen with your ears, listen with your mind. No, don't listen with your mind, but listen with your spirit. Listening stops with the ears, the mind stops with recognition, but spirit is empty and waits on all things. The Way gathers in emptiness alone. Emptiness is the fasting of the mind."

### The Groove and the Rut

> "Over the decades, I have observed many brilliant minds who have had success that emerged from curiosity and dynamism, and then have been tempted to spend the rest of their lives replicating the solutions they arrived at. The problem is that replication is static while creation is dynamically attuned to the NOW."

Neural pathways form. We build grooves for water to flow. This is healthy—but beware of grooves becoming ruts, where water stops and becomes stagnant.

**The key insight:** "The act of creation of the framework is the superpower, not the framework itself."

### Gumption

A state embracing discovery—creatively alive, curious, present, can-do, deep caring about what one is doing right now. Fueled by "psychic gasoline" from attunement to Dynamic Quality.

> "Gumption drives Caring, connects you to your work on a visceral level, helps you live stoked, filled with enthusiasm, deeply present to nuance, filled with wonder."

Caring without Gumption drains you. Gumption without Caring is restless energy looking for somewhere to land.

## The Art of Training: Key Passages

### The Four Pillars

1. **Fall in Love** — The starting point
2. **Go All In** — "Become what the people in armchairs would call 'obsessive' but in the Arena feels sublime."
3. **Master the Skills** — Mindset, Learning, Creativity, Performance Psychology
4. **Connect** — Learn the macro from the micro, the many from the one

But the order is malleable. Sometimes going all-in sparks the love. Sometimes it's the process of self-discovery itself.

### Inner Sage, Inner Child

> "Most coaches teach the way they learned—in the same order, through the same modalities. With a bias toward their way of perceiving the world. Dancing around their unseen inhibitions. Imposing the same ceilings on level that they ran into themselves."

The child learning to surf: figures out how to pop up, plays between stances, freedom from "the right way," dancing on the board. The adult: "What technique should I use? What should I impose on my body and this board and this unruly ocean to give me the illusion of control?"

### Embracing Your Funk

> "What are the quirky ways your mind works that teachers, parents or coaches have tried to normalize? What are the seams in your being that conformists want to iron out?... What if the funkiness you have felt pressured to repress is the key to unlocking something? The seeds of your future virtuosity."

### The Cave

When starting a new chapter—go to a place without external stimulus. Let internal waters still. Imagine possibility without imposing execution constraints. Free from reactivity and inertia.

> "What is the goal? To calm one's system so profoundly that we can see into our depths. Not to settle for the visibility of our normal choppy waters, or even for the ripples that we can relax into after weeks in the cave... but for that magical glass-over that happens on the ocean from time to time..."

Our identity gives us footing—and impedes imagining what's possible beyond familiar patterns. When constructs disappear, we feel vertigo. Can we embrace it? This is when the discovery of self-expression really begins.

## The Celtics/Mazzulla Narrative

Josh's work with Joe Mazzulla and the Boston Celtics, detailed in the new draft:

### The Cave Process (Post ECF Loss 2023)

Joe came to the Costa Rica jungle fresh after a heartbreaking Game 7 loss. The goal: liberate him from the leverage, brilliance, and burden of the Celtics legacy. Free from concerns about external perception, fears of failure, expectations.

Blue sky thinking without execution constraints—letting imagination layer dreams on top of dreams.

> "Perhaps counterintuitively, I have found that the impossible cubed is often much more possible than what first appears to be the merely impossible."

The principles that emerged from pure self-expression:
- Choose Love and Joy
- Figure it Out
- Care Fiercely
- Be Gratitude
- Commit and Compete
- Hunt Adversity

### The Kiskadee Story

Male Kiskadee bird sitting alone in nest above eggs. Suddenly surrounded by 40+ rowdy Howler monkeys, leaping between trees, chaos everywhere.

Josh asked Joe: "If you were that Kiskadee, what would you do?"

Without hesitation: "Eat the Eggs."

"What??"

"If my babies are going to die, better for me to do it myself."

**What the bird actually did:** Flew straight up, dive bombed the alpha male (not smaller, vulnerable targets), nailed the alpha on the head three times. Alpha screamed, fled. Entire troop followed their leader. Problem solved with one decisive flurry.

**The insight about Joe:** His fatalism. Deep peace and resilience in faith that a greater power has a plan—but in his first year, sometimes he'd intuit a game was lost and not do everything to reverse the tide. Not proactive with timeouts. Had not learned to transfer energy to a team in downward spiral.

This became the foundation for "Hunt Adversity" and "Predator Mindset."

### The Triangle of Death (2024 Finals)

Joe's strategy against Luka Doncic and the Dallas Mavericks: systematically break down the bonds between Luka and Kyrie, then between both and their role players.

Jaylen Brown's execution: relentlessly in Luka's grill physically—but giving him psychological outs. "Luka can't do it on his own. He's so good but they aren't giving him the ball." Speaking about Luka so Luka could overhear, never directly to him (which would make him stronger).

The cracks appeared in Game 3. Luka's frustration with teammates. Grimaces and gestures. Boston won the championship.

## Solitaire Chess / Firewalking

Josh's method of studying Fischer:

Set up pieces, display only one move at a time. Play the opponent's move, then sit 1-5 minutes—really wrestle with the decision. Only after making your own choice, continue.

> "Usually I would choose the same move as Fischer. Sometimes I would not. Those moments that I would not were future gold."

The game takes hours. Mind tired from countless complex decisions. "I had become part of the battle. My understanding was visceral because I had suffered through the navigation of huge complexities."

Only after this firewalking, read Fischer's commentary. "A balm of deep and potent understanding that would soothe the little micro-abrasions in my mind."

> "The key for me to soak in the simplicity on the other side of complexity, was to travel through the complexity myself. I had to sweat for the wisdom."

## The Many from the One

Why don't more strong chess players translate to other disciplines?

**Answer:** Localized learning. Localized teaching.

The bishop vs. knight lesson—most learn in chess-specific language. But from lesson one, Josh would discuss:
- How nothing has static or intrinsic value—everything depends on relationship to other things
- How much more valuable is water in drought vs. abundance?
- How does the same 80-degree pool feel jumping from hot tub vs. cold plunge?

**Universal principles from two chess pieces:**
- Relativity
- Contextuality
- Interdependence
- Dynamic vs. Static Quality
- Flexibility of Mind
- Situational Awareness
- Cognitive biases
- Tolerance of cognitive dissonance
- Mutability
- Transience
- The illusion of certainty

> "Each of these could be a connecting fiber between chess and life, between the micro and the macro. The tragedy is, so few chess students are taught this way. And so their relationship to chess becomes localized. Trapped inside the walls of the 64 squares."

## Deliberate Practice for the Stoked Mind (DPSM)

What is deliberate practice?
- Deconstruction of component parts
- Training each separately before bringing together
- Feedback loop for review
- Refinement based on feedback
- Loop continues

**What is DPSM?**

> "The same process, but ignited by Fire. Passion. Love for Art. Wildly deep presence. A sense of beauty for the subtle ripples of life. Awe. Full Physiological engagement."

The philosophologists, academics, critics don't understand DPSM. "Because they won't get in ice water. Bleed for their arts. Train to love training. Or experience the bliss beyond deep internal resistance. But that is where all the juice starts flowing."

## The Arena: Dynamic Quality in Competition

### Two Timelines

> "One way to visualize a chess battle is that there are two timelines. One is the player's minds. One is what is happening with the pieces on the board. When those two time lines are in synch, you are present, like a hunter sensing the jungle or the water, tapped into Dynamic Quality. But the moment you think about winning or losing or how you look from the outside, your mind slips into the static and the fight continues without you. That's when you get your ass kicked."

### The 2004 Push Hands World Championship Finals

Up in the first round. Up 3 points in the second. Opponent's heel right on the edge of the ring.

> "I had the thought, one inch to the world championship. My mind slipped from the Dynamic to the Static. And the exact second I had that thought my face hit the ground."

**Closest to winning, closest to losing.**

### Predator Mindset

> "When you are in the water with sharks that start to amp up, get aggressive... In that moment if you emit fear they will sense the vibration and things will keep escalating. But what I learned to do is look for something to kill. I started hunting. And when my intention became that of a hunter, I was one of them, and the pack's body language changed completely."

The Prevent Defense Mentality: A team dominates to build a lead, then shifts to protecting it. Lay back, become defensive, kill time. Allow opponent to regain composure, confidence, momentum.

> "I think you have to eliminate the Prevent Defense Mentality, and all its cousins, from the psychological lexicon of this team... The proactive framing is PREDATOR MINDSET."

## The MIQ (Most Important Question)

> "One of the key distinguishing strengths of great thinkers is a sense for where to look. What matters? The stronger chess player does not look at more—this is a misperception. In fact, he often looks at less. She looks in the right direction."

The practice:
1. Maintain questions throughout the day
2. Evening: identify the MIQ—where the knife meets resistance, where freedom from stuckness would reveal horizons
3. Pose to the unconscious
4. Release it—don't try to resolve
5. Morning, pre-input: return and journal

> "Over time, with this practice oceans of insight and potency will surface. Interestingly, your dream recall and MIQ breakthroughs will likely improve together."

## Marcelo Garcia: The Entanglement

Marcelo—9x world champion, arguably the greatest grappler pound for pound. A low rep somatic learner who absorbs lessons so fast he doesn't need to be shown again. His entire knowledge is pre-conceptual, elemental.

The entanglement with dysfunction:

> "I memorize all my pain. I never forget it. I carry it all with me every moment."

Every insult, every wrong glance, every raised voice, every throw, sweep, choke—he can't let any of it go. The very thing that sets him apart as a learner and gladiator ravages him every moment.

### The ADCC Evolution

After winning 2007 ADCC with his back game, Marcelo shed the repertoire like a snake skin. The day after winning, on the mats developing the omoplata game he'd win with two years later. The whole BJJ world studied what he won with—while he'd already left it behind.

## On Coaching

### The Art of Being the Mentee

> "There is a wild difference between a student who just extracts value and a student who listens with deep presence and gratitude, thinks, integrates, creates—and comes back with more. Who turns a fertile seed into a plant overnight and a garden a week later... This inspires the teacher to reveal the inner game, the secrets earned through blood and tears, that should not just be given away to the student who is undeserving."

### Attunement

> "One of the most important traits of great leaders and mentors is a sense of attunement to Quality, both in themselves and in their people. The feeling of when I am on, and when I am off. And when you are on, when less so... in a room of 15 or 20 or 50 people, it becomes like a simultaneous exhibition in chess... you can still feel when someone in the corner of the room is distracted, is coming from a bias pattern, some political driver, stuckness... when there is a glitch in the matrix."

### What Not To Do

- Finish their sentences, literally or figuratively
- Rush to influence because of ego or insecurity
- Speak from outside their river
- Be rigid about any plan or system
- Be prescriptive
- Assume what works for you will work for them
- Guide from self-interest
- Grip any framework, label, or diagnostic tightly
- Allow your presence to slip from Dynamic to Static Quality
- Become static yourself

## Mindset Evolution

Josh's journey through distinct phases:

- **Age 6**: Innocent floodgates—learning felt like remembering
- **Age 7-8**: Pre-conscious competitor—unobstructed, invincible
- **Age 8-9**: First heartbreak—"The greatest thing that ever happened in my chess career."
- **Age 9-15**: Freedom, love, self-expression
- **Age 15-18**: Self-consciousness—the movie, the spotlight, Russian coach urging opposite style
- **Age 18-21**: Searching—Slovenia, existentialist literature
- **Age 21-23**: Self and Other—Columbia, East Asian Philosophy
- **Age 23-28**: Post-conscious competitor—Tai Chi, fighting
- **Age 29-35**: Beginner's mind—BJJ with Marcelo
- **Age 35-37**: Rupture—broke back one week before becoming a father
- **Age 37-49**: Foiling, coaching, "the search becoming increasingly internal, natural, receptive, interconnected"

## Slow is Fast

Soviet chess discovery for players making sloppy errors:

Switch order from: Decide → Make move → Write move down
To: Decide → Write move down → Look back at board → Take a breath → Make move

Reduced unforced errors 90%+.

> "The resurfacing reveals the obvious to the mind that is like a hand reaching deep into a cupboard and missing what it is searching for right in front."

Applied: sleeping on MIQs, revisiting decisions in multiple physiological states, re-reading an email for 10 seconds before sending.

## Key Tensions

**Static vs. Dynamic**: Frameworks help but also cage. The moment you grip something too tightly, it loses aliveness.

**External vs. Internal**: The undulation between outer technical work and inner refinement.

**Self-expression vs. Stretch**: Being yourself versus pushing beyond comfortable limits.

**Revealing vs. Protecting**: Choose the easy round to rest, or the toughest partner when destroyed.

**Predator vs. Prey**: The mindset shift that can cost or win championships.

---

*The throughline: virtuosity emerges from the marriage of fierce commitment and lightness of grip, technical precision and intuitive flow, self-expression and continuous growth. The path is carved, not followed. The wave washes the sand painting away. Begin again.*
